Spanish bread
Jump to recipe!
TIME
3 hours
YIELD
16 pieces
Spanish bread are soft and fluffy bread rolls, filled with a sweet filling made of brown sugar and breadcrumbs. The bread of Spanish bread has a texture that is quite similar to that of pandesal, and it is also coated with breadcrumbs upon baking. Spanish bread is an amazing merienda snack, and when partnered with some coffee or hot chocolate is just, *chef’s kiss*.
What you need
Just like most breads, you’ll need some equipment to help in kneading. It’s best to have a bench scraper on hand if you’re going to knead your dough by hand. But, if you have a stand mixer or even a hand mixer on hand, then definitely use them since they’ll ease up the kneading process by a lot. You will also need a rolling pin or anything that does the same job, like a wine bottle, to roll out your dough once you shape it.
Some extra tips for making spanish bread!
Once you roll the Spanish bread to make its classic twist, make sure to close the seams properly! If you leave the edges of your bread open, then the filling is bound to ooze out when you bake your bread. Aside from that, when you add your filling to the bread, add more than you think you need. During my first attempts with making Spanish bread, I usually find that I put too little filling inside, so always put much more than you think you need. If you’re worried about it being too sweet, trust me, I thought the same and every single time I added extra filling, it always turned out just right:)
Monay Recipe
INGREDIENTS
  • 3 ¼ cup all purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 4 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tsp instant yeast
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • 1 egg
  • 1/3 cup unsalted butter

  • ½ cup unsalted butter (softened)
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • 1/3 cup condensed milk
  • ¾ cup bread crumbs
  1. Mix together the flour, salt, sugar, and yeast.
  2. After, add in the milk and egg.
  3. Mix until it forms a dough, then knead for about 3 mins. Then, add in your softened butter and continue kneading.
  4. Knead the dough until it has fully developed and passes the windowpane test (check image below). This will take about 10 mins using a stand mixer, or 30 mins by hand.
  5. Let the dough rise for 1 hour, or until the dough has doubled in size.
  6. While waiting for the dough to rise, make the filling. First, mix together the softened butter and brown sugar until fully combined.
  7. Next add in your condensed milk. Lastly, mix in the breadcrumbs, then set aside the filling.
  8. Divide the dough into 16 equal pieces. This is usually best done with a weighing scale so that you can ensure each piece is equal. Shape each of the pieces into a ball.
  9. Flatten each piece into an oval and spread about 1-2 tbsp of your filling. Roll it into a log, close the seam, and tuck in the sides.
  10. After adding the filling, dredge each piece in breadcrumbs. Let the dough rise for another 30 mins.
  11. Bake the bread in a preheated oven at 180°C for 18-20 minutes.
  12. Let the bread cool down once done, then serve and enjoy!
