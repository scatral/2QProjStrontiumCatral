Banana cake
Jump to recipe!
TIME
1 hour
YIELD
1 loaf
Banana cake is a classic moist and sweet bread and is a go-to treat to make when you have overripe bananas at home. Now, you may be wondering why I call it banana cake instead of banana bread. The process of making this treat is actually much more similar to how you would make a cake instead of a bread, hence why I call it a cake! Either way, banana cake/bread is one of the more basic treats that you can make at home, and is a quick and easy solution to any sweet craving that you have:)
What you need
Banana cake is honestly a very straightforward recipe and doesn’t need any special equipment. All you really need are some measuring cups and spoons, a bowl, and a loaf pan or cake pan, then you’re good to go!
Some extra tips for making banana cake!
Banana cake is a very versatile recipe, which is why you should feel free to add any toppings or extra components to make your banana cake be to your liking! Chocolate chips, nuts, and even streusel go very well with banana cake, so you can never go wrong with adding some:) I personally like to drizzle in some caramel sauce into the batter, and top of the cake right before baking.
Banana Cake Recipe
INGREDIENTS
  • 3 overripe bananas (mashed)
  • 2 eggs
  • 3/8 cup white sugar
  • 3/8 cup brown sugar
  • 3/4 cup oil
  • 1/3 cup milk
  • 2 tbsp plain yogurt
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 1/2 cup all purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp nutmeg
  • 1/2 tsp cloves
  1. Whisk together eggs, sugar, and oil until well combined.
  2. Add in milk, yogurt, vanilla extract, and salt.
  3. Sift in flour, baking powder, baking soda, and spices.
  4. Fold in mashed banana (make sure to not overmix!)
  5. Pour into a lined 8-inch cake pan, and bake in oven preheated to 170°C for 35-40 minutes.
  6. Once done baking, let the bread cool for about 10 minutes. Enjoy!
