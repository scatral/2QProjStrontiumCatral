Bread
Monay
Jump to recipe!
TIME
2 hours
YIELD
8 pieces
Monay is a classic Filipino bread roll that is dense and is characterized by its large indentation in the center. It can be found in most bakeries in the Philippines, and it has a slightly sweet and milky taste to it. It only uses a few basic ingredients, and tastes heavenly when partnered with some butter or jam!
What you need
Monay dough can be hard to handle at times, given that it is quite dense. If you’re kneading by hand, a bench scraper will always help to easily gather all of your dough. But, if you have a stand mixer on hand, then definitely use that since it will ease up the kneading process tenfold. Aside from this, you'll just need other basic equipment like measuring cups, an oven, etc.
Some extra tips for making monay!
There will be times when your dough will be sticky or hard to work with, and most people will resort to adding flour to the dough to make it less sticky. However, with monay dough (and a lot of other doughs), using oil instead of flour is much much better. Incorporating more flour into whatever your making can significantly change the outcome if you add too much. I personally like to add any neutral-flavored oil or olive oil to my hands and the dough to make the it more manageable.
Monay Recipe
INGREDIENTS
  • 3/4 cup water (room temp.)
  • 1 egg
  • 1/3 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup milk powder
  • 2 tsp instant yeast
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 3 1/4 cups all purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp salt

  • 1 beaten egg (for egg wash)
  1. Mix together the water, egg, sugar, milk powder, and instant yeast.
  2. After, add in the oil, flour, and salt.
  3. Knead the dough until it has fully developed and passes the windowpane test (check image below). This will take about 10 mins using a stand mixer, or 30 mins by hand.
  4. Let the dough rise for 1 hour, or until the dough has doubled in size.
  5. Divide the dough into 8 equal pieces. This is usually best done with a weighing scale so that you can ensure each piece is equal.
  6. Role of the 8 pieces into balls, and let them rise for about 30 mins.
  7. Preheat your oven to 180°C.
  8. While waiting, cut slits at the top of each piece using scissors, and brush your egg wash on top.
  9. Bake your bread for 18 mins, and once done, place them on a cooling rack and enjoy!
