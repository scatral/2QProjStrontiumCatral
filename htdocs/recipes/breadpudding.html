Bread pudding
Jump to recipe!
TIME
1 hour
YIELD
8"x 8" dish
Bread pudding is soft and light treat, that has a wonderful custard-like taste. Getting to bake bread pudding for the family is probably the only reason having stale bread at home is good:) It is very good in its classic version but is very versatile too as it can be customized with flavor, spices and toppings according to what you have at home or just feel like eating for the day. Though it’s not a very common treat here, trust me when I say bread pudding tastes absolutely amazing, and is very easy to make!
What you need
Making bread pudding is very simple, hence why you’ll only need a couple of basic equipment to make it. Some measuring cups and spoons, an 8x8 baking dish (or any similar size), and a saucepan are all you really need to make some good bread pudding:)
Some extra tips for making egg pie!
Bread pudding gets most of its flavor from the sauce that you’re going to make, so make sure you do this part carefully because it could make or break your pudding! Aside from this, make sure you use stale bread when making bread pudding. The drier your bread is, the more liquid it can soak up, which makes for a better bread pudding! If you don’t have any stale bread, you can always use fresh bread, and just bake it in an oven to dry it up before making the bread pudding itself.
Bread Pudding Recipe
INGREDIENTS
  • 9-12 slices stale bread
  • 3 tbsp butter (melted)
  • ½ cup raisins (Optional)
  • 4 eggs, beaten
  • 2 cups milk
  • ¾ cup white sugar
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • ½ tsp nutmeg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2 tbsp flour
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar (packed)
  • 2 cups milk (hot)
  • 1 tbsp vanilla extract
  • 1 pinch salt
  1. Get your bread and break it into smaller pieces that are about 1 inch in size. Place them in your baking dish.
  2. Drizzle the melted butter over the bread and add some raisins on top if desired.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the eggs, milk, sugar, spices, and vanilla extract. Make sure to whisk the mixture well.
  4. Pour the liquid over the bread and lightly push down on the bread, ensuring that each piece is soaked with the egg mixture.
  5. Bake in a preheated oven at 175°C for 45 mins, or until the custard is fully cooked and no longer liquid-y.
  6. While waiting for the bread pudding, prepare the sauce. In a saucepan, melt your butter over medium heat. Whisk in the flour and let it simmer for about 1 min, making sure that you stir continuously.
  7. Slowly pour in the heated-up milk while whisking, and then at the brown sugar and salt. Continue to stir and cook the mixture until it starts to thicken.
  8. Remove from heat, and then add the vanilla extract.
  9. Pour the sauce over the bread pudding while it’s warm, then serve and enjoy!
