Chicken Fingers
Jump to recipe!
TIME
30 mins
YIELD
12 pieces
Crispy chicken fingers that are perfectly fried and juicy are one of my family's favorite things to eat for lunch, and they happen to be incredibly easy to make. Whether you eat them with rice, dipped in some ranch, or cut up and mixed into a salad, you can nevergo wrong with making a quick batch of chicken fingers, and it only uses a few ingredients!
What you need
Making chicken fingers is very straightforward, and all you need is a couple of bowls for your breading, and a pan to fry the chicken:)
Some extra tips for making chicken fingers!
Most of the steps to make chicken fingers are pretty simple, but the part where you'll most likely have problems with is frying the chicken, specifically dealing with popping oil. I absolutely hate oil popping whenever I cook, but a trick that I have found have helped with controlling this is making sure to reduce the moisture of whatever your frying, so try your best to coat each piece properly and don't leave any spots uncoated with the breadcrumb mixture.
Chicken Fingers Recipe
INGREDIENTS
  • 1/2 kg chicken breast
  • 1/3 cup all purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 1/2 tsp ground pepper
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  1. Cut chicken breat into about 1 1/2 inch-thick slices.
  2. Mix together flour, 1/2 tsp salt, and 1/2 tsp ground pepper.
  3. In a separate bowl, beat your egg.
  4. In one last bowl, mix together breadcrumbs, grated Parmesan, 1 tsp salt, and 1 tsp ground pepper.
  5. Dip each chicken slice into flour mixture, egg, and breadcrumb mixture, in that order.
  6. Add some oil to your pan (until the bottom of it is mostly covered) and let it heat up.
  7. Once oil is hot enough, fry the chicken fingers until golden brown.
  8. Repeat as needed, then serve and enjoy!
