Garlic cream cheese bread
Jump to recipe!
TIME
2 hours
YIELD
8 pieces
Cream cheese garlic bread became more popular among Filipinos along with the rise of K-dramas and K-pop fans; and we are just grateful because this popular Korean street food takes garlic bread to a whole new level of deliciousness! It’s basically the same soft and fluffy garlic bread but slathered with a sweet and savory cream cheese filling that will definitely make you want for more.
What you need
This bread generally just needs your basic tools for breadmaking, like a bench scraper or stand mixer. The only extra tool you will need is a piping bag or anything that does the same job, like a Ziploc bag. You’ll need this when you pipe in the filling for the bread, as using a bag to add it in is so much easier and more convenient that placing it in with a spoon or spatula.
Some extra tips for making garlic cream cheese bread!
When making your garlic butter dip, try to do it in a narrow and deep dish. Dipping your bread into the butter mixture will be much easier if you use a dish like this, given that you’ll be able to dip it in deeper and coat a lot of the bread at once.
Garlic Cream Cheese Bread Recipe
INGREDIENTS
  • 1 ¾ cup bread flour
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 1 tsp yeast
  • 1 egg
  • ½ cup milk
  • 1/8 cup unsalted butter (softened)
  • Milk (for milk wash)
  • Shredded cheese (as needed)

  • 200g cream cheese (softened)
  • 100g butter (softened)
  • 40g sugar

  • 1 egg
  • 20g milk/cream
  • 50g melted butter
  • 40g honey
  • 5 cloves of garlic (minced)
  • 2 tbsp mayo
  • 2 tbsp chopped parsley/basil
  1. Whisk together the flour, sugar, salt, and yeast.
  2. Add your egg and milk. Mix together until your ingredients start to form a dough, then knead it for about 3 mins.
  3. After quickly kneading your dough, add in your softened butter, and then continue to knead for about 10 – 30 mins (this will depend on whether or not your kneading by hand or with a stand mixer). Use the windowpane test to check whether your dough has been kneaded enough (check image below).
  4. Once the dough is done being kneaded, place it in a greased bowl and let it rise for 1 hour.
  5. Punch the air out of your risen dough, divide it into 8 pieces, and shape each piece into a ball. Let the individual pieces rise again for 15 mins.
  6. Once done rising, roll each piece into an oval shape and shape it into logs, making sure you close them edges. Let these rise again for another hour.
  7. Brush some milk onto the top of the dough, and sprinkle some shredded cheese on top. Then, bake your bread in a preheated oven at 180°C for 13-15 minutes. Let the bread cool once done.
  8. While waiting for the bread, prepare the cream cheese filling. In a bowl, mix together your softened cream cheese, butter, and sugar. Place the mixture in a piping bag or Ziploc so that you can easily pipe it onto your bread later.
  9. In a separate bowl, mix together your egg, milk/cream, melted butter, honey, minced garlic, mayo, and chopped parsley/basil. This will be the garlic butter dip for the bread.
  10. Once your bread has cooled down, make an opening at the center of it with a pair of scissors. Pipe your cream cheese filling inside the opening, adding in however much you want.
  11. Next, dip your bread into the garlic butter mixture that you made previously, making sure to coat all sides.
  12. Place your bread back onto your baking tray, and bake for just 5 mins at 150°C. Serve and enjoy once done, as this is best eaten while it’s still warm:)
