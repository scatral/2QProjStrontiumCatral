Baked sea bass
Jump to recipe!
TIME
24 1/2 hours
YIELD
1 fish
Sea bass, better known as Apahap, is a common fish eaten in the Philippines and other parts of the world. Nothing beats perfectly cooked fish that is full of flavor, and partner that up with the fact that you don't have to deal with popping oil, then you got a killer fish recipe! Baked sea bass is not only flavorful, but is also a healthy source of protein during meals, which is why you should definitely give it a shot:)
What you need
You'll need a couple of things to be able to pull of this recipe, including an oven that's big enough to fit a whole fish, any container where you can leave the fish to marinade in the fridge, and a blender to make the marinade for the fish.
Some extra tips for making baked sea bass!
You can use this marinade for other types of fish like tilapia or fillets, just adjust on the cooking time and cooking proportions:) Also, in case you want your fish to have a nice browning finish, choose the broiler setting in your oven or turn the heat up on the last 3-5mins of your baking time. On the other hand, if your fish is already browned but not yet cooked through, cover it with foil to avoid the skin from getting burned.

Lastly, if you don't have enough time to marinade the fish, make some extra marinade mix. Heat it up over the stove for a side sauce for your fish.
Baked Sea Bass Recipe
INGREDIENTS
  • 1 medium-sized Apahap (scaled and gutted)
  • 1 lemon
  • salt and pepper (to taste)
  • ½ bunch parsley
  • ½ bunch basil
  • 1 small onion
  • 1 small tomato
  • 4-5 garlic cloves
  • ½-1 cup water or oil
  • 1-2 cups cherry tomatoes
  1. Make 3-4 diagonal cuts all the way to the bone on each side of the fish.
  2. Squeeze some lemon over the fish, then season with salt and pepper.
  3. Next, chop up the parsley, basil, tomato, garlic, and 1/2 of the onion.
  4. Add the chopped up ingredients to a blender or food processor. Blend it while adding water/oil until a puree is formed.
  5. Pour the marinade over the fish, making sure to coat both sides, inside and out.
  6. Leave the fish to marinade, ideally for 24 hours.
  7. After marinating, place the fish on a lined baking pan. Drizzle 2 tbsp of oil, and add the cherry tomatoes and the other half of your onion (sliced).
  8. Bake in a preheated oven at 230°C for 15-20 minutes, depending on the size of your fish.
