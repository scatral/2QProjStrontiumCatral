Egg pie
Jump to recipe!
TIME
3 hours
YIELD
9" pie
Egg pie is a sweet Filipino pie, with a custard filling and flaky crust. They are mostly sold in Filipino bakeries in individual slices. Egg pie actually does not taste like eggs at all, and can actually be more attributed to the taste of the flan part of leche flan! It can mostly be recognized by the burnt layer on the top of it, which is actually egg whites.
What you need
Egg pie doesn’t need any incredibly specialized equipment, but you definitely need to have a pie pan or any dish of a similar shape. A rolling pin is a must as well, or any long and cylindrical object around the house like a wine bottle, so you can easily roll out your pie crust and get an even width. It would also help to have a hand mixer or stand mixer, because you’re going to have to whip up a lot of egg whites, so you’ll be in for a workout if you try to do it manually!

Aside from that, you will also need to cut your butter into your dough (more info in recipe portion). You can do this with your hands, but you can also use a pastry cutter or fork in order to ease up the process.
Some extra tips for making egg pie!
Make sure that the butter that you’re going to use is cold! This is a must with all pie crusts, because this is what makes them flaky and butter-y. The same goes for when your rolling out your dough, because if your dough gets warm while you roll it out into the pan, the butter will melt, the dough will be hard to handle, and your crust won’t end up having the best texture.
Egg pie Recipe
INGREDIENTS
  • 1 ½ cup all purpose flour
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • ½ cup unsalted butter (cold and cubed)
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 3-4 tbsp ice water
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract

  • 1 can of evaporated milk (370ml)
  • 4 whole eggs
  • 5/6 cup of sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 egg white
  1. For crust: Mix together flour, salt, and sugar.
  2. Next, cut in the butter into the dry ingredients. Cutting butter into dough is basically adding your butter into the dry ingredients, and pinching the butter to incorporate it into the mixture. However, you can also use a pastry cutter or fork, and press down on the butter to break it down into smaller pieces. (See video below for reference)
  3. Once your butter has been broken down into pebble-like sizes, you can now add the egg yolk, ice water, and vanilla extract. Mix until the dough is formed. You can add an extra tablespoon of ice water if it doesn’t form yet.
  4. Form your dough into a ball, cover in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.
  5. After chilling your dough, roll it out and place it onto your pan. Cut out any excess. Place the pan back into the fridge.
  6. For filling: While the crust chills, make the filling. Heat up the evaporated milk in a pot on low heat.
  7. While you heat up the milk, add the eggs and sugar in a bowl and whisk them together.
  8. Mix in the heated-up evaporated milk and vanilla extract with your eggs and sugar mixture.
  9. Next, get your egg white and beat it until you have stiff peaks.
  10. Fold in the beaten egg white into the evaporated milk mixture.
  11. Pour the filling into your chilled crust and bake in a 170°C preheated oven for 15 minutes.
  12. After, continue baking it at 160°C for about 30-40 mins, or until the filling is no longer jiggly.
  13. Let it cool, serve, and enjoy!
