Apple pie
Jump to recipe!
TIME
3 hours
YIELD
9" pie
Apple pie is a popular American dessert though some of the earliest recipes were from England. The main filling are apples, mixed with spices and baked with crusts at the bottom and top. It is also usually served with ice cream or whipped cream.
What you need
Apple pie doesn’t need any incredibly specialized equipment, but you definitely need to have a pie pan or any dish of a similar shape (specifically one that is about 9 inches for this recipe). A rolling pin is a must as well, or any long and cylindrical object around the house like a wine bottle, so you can easily roll out your pie crust and get an even width. Moreover, if you’re going to go with a lattice pattern for the top of your crust like the one in the given pictures, you have to have a pizza cutter or any sharp knife on hand.
Some extra tips for making apple pie!
An essential part of the process that you need to take note of is ensuring that your butter is cold when you make the dough, and your dough isn’t too warm when you roll it out onto your pan so that the butter won’t melt.

Aside from this, if you’re a beginner in pie making, then you may want to opt out of making a lattice pattern for the top of your pie. Instead, you can just roll out the pie dough into a normal circle, and fully cover the top of your pie without any pattern.
Apple pie Recipe
INGREDIENTS
  • 2 ½ cups all purpose flour
  • ½ tbsp sugar
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 cup of unsalted butter (cold, and sliced into cubes)
  • 7-8 tbsp ice water
  • 1 beaten egg + 1 tbsp water (for egg wash)

  • 4-5 Fuji apples (peeled, cored, and thinly sliced)
  • 1 ½ tsp cinnamon
  • 8 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 3 tbsp all purpose flour
  • ¼ cup water
  • 5/6 cup of sugar
  1. For crust: Mix together the flour, sugar, and salt in a bowl.
  2. Next, cut in the butter into the dry ingredients. Cutting butter into dough is basically adding your butter into the dry ingredients and pinching the butter to incorporate it into the mixture. However, you can also use a pastry cutter or fork, and press down on the butter to break it down into smaller pieces. (See video below for reference)
  3. Once your butter has been broken down into pebble-like sizes, you can now add the ice water. Mix until the dough is formed. You can add an extra tablespoon of the water if it doesn’t form yet.
  4. Form your dough into a ball, cover in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.
  5. For filling: While the dough chills, make the filling. First, melt the butter in a saucepan over medium heat.
  6. Whisk in the flour and let it simmer for about 1 minute, making sure to mix continuously.
  7. Add your water and sugar and bring to a boil. Then, reduce the heat and let it simmer for an extra 3 minutes. Remove from heat once done.
  8. Get your sliced apples and sprinkle the cinnamon on top. Mix together until the apple is fully coated with cinnamon.
  9. Pour the sauce your previously made over the apples and mix together until well combined.
  10. Roll out half of your dough until about 12 inches, and place on your pie pan. Then, add your filling.
  11. Roll out the second half of your dough and place it on top of your filling, making sure to seal the edges of the crust properly. You can also do a lattice pattern instead of just directly placing your dough (see apple pie image).
  12. Brush the top with the egg wash and bake in a preheated oven at 210°C for 15 minutes, then reduce to 175°C and continue baking for about 45 minutes, or until apples are soft.
  13. Let it cool for about 1 hour, serve, and enjoy!
