Pasta
Truffle pasta
Jump to recipe!
TIME
20 mins
YIELD
8 servings
Truffle pasta is like an ordinary cream-based pasta, but with a kick of truffle-flavor that gives it a very strong and earthy aroma and taste. Though truffle can be an acquired taste, you should definitely give it a try because aside from the fact that it is quick and easy to make, it also makes for a very good dinner when partnered with some meat and veg:)
What you need
To make some truffle pasta, you'll need a relatively large pot to cook your pasta in and a saucepan to make the cream. Of course, you'll also need to have some truffle oil to give the pasta its truffle flavor.
Some extra tips for making truffle pasta!
If you really aren't a big fan of truffle, then feel free to reduce or even completely remove the truffle oil. The pasta will still taste good, and will be similar to something like carbonara. With that, you can also add other ingredients to the recipe to make it to your liking. Feel free to experiment with proteins (ham, prosciutto, shrimp, etc.) or veggies (spinach, mushroom, etc.).

Also, boil your pasta in salt water and some oil. For spaghetti, 11 mins is the optimal time to get that al dente finish.
Truffle Pasta Recipe
INGREDIENTS
  • 1/4 kg of spaghetti or angel hair pasta
  • 2/3 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 cup culinary or all-purpose cream
  • 1/4 cup grated fresh parmesan
  • 3 tbsp truffle oil
  • grated parmesan (for topping)
  • freshly cracked pepper
  1. Cook the pasta according to package instructions.
  2. In a pan, melt the butter until bubbly.
  3. Add in your cream then quickly turn off. Continue stirring until fully incorporated.
  4. Add the pasta in. Sprinkle some parmesan cheese then put the truffle oil.
  5. Season with salt and pepper and make sure everything is well combined.
  6. Serve immediately.
