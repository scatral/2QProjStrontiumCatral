Red bean bread
Jump to recipe!
TIME
2 1/2 hours
YIELD
6 pieces
Red bean bread is a sweet and fluffy bun filled with, you guessed it, red bean! Mostly found in East Asia, red bean bread is more commonly known as ‘Anpan’ and originated from Japan. The filling, which is known as ‘Anko’ is a red bean paste which is made through boiling, mashing, and sweetening red beans. The taste is quite similar to Filipino monggo bread, with the bread itself being very soft and fluffy, and the filling being sweet and slightly grainy because of the beans.
What you need
Red bean bread dough is actually quite easy to handle, so you won’t have any issues there. However, like the name suggests, you are going to need some red bean paste. I personally buy my own red bean paste instead of making it from scratch since red beans aren’t that common here in the Philippines. I use the one from the brand ‘Bakerdream’, which honestly tastes incredible, and is what is most commonly found in physical and online baking stores. You will also need a rolling pin for when you roll out and shape your dough (check part below for more info).
Some extra tips for making red bean bread!
When I make red bean bread, I use a method that involves scoring the dough in a specific way. This part isn’t really needed, and I mostly just do it for aesthetic purposes. If you’re a beginner in bread-making and don’t think you can do this part properly, you can definitely just make the bread like a normal bun and simply wrap the dough around the paste (see picture below).
If you are going to slice and twist it like what I normally do, make sure you have a sharp knife and that you put flour or oil on your workspace to avoid making your dough stick onto your counter when you roll it.
Red Bean Bread Recipe
INGREDIENTS
  • 1 3/8 cup all purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 1 egg
  • 3/8 cup milk
  • 1 tsp yeast
  • 1/8 cup unsalted butter (softened)
  • 150g red bean paste
  • 1 beaten egg (for egg wash)
  1. Mix together the flour, sugar, and salt.
  2. Then, add in the egg, milk, and yeast. Mix together until a dough has formed, then continue kneading for about 3 mins.
  3. After quickly kneading your dough, add in your softened butter, and then continue to knead for about 10 – 30 mins (this will depend on whether or not your kneading by hand or with a stand mixer). Use the windowpane test to check whether your dough has been kneaded enough (check image below).
  4. Once done kneading, let your dough rise for about 1 hour, or until doubled in size.
  5. After rising, punch out the air from your dough and divide it into 6 equal pieces. Shape them into balls and let them rise for another 10 mins.
  6. While the dough continues to rise, divide your red bean paste into 25g balls.
  7. After the individual pieces of dough are done rising, flatten it out in order to form a circle, and place a ball of your red bean paste in the center. Wrap the paste with your dough to form a ball, making sure to close the seams.
  8. Optional: if you wish to just make plain red bean buns without the design shows in the photos, then you can stop here. However, if you wish to follow it then do steps 9 – 10:
  9. Roll out your ball of dough into a long oval, then score the top of it with a knife. Make sure to control the pressure that you are applying, ensuring that you aren’t cutting through the whole dough and just scoring the top to expose some of the filling.
  10. Flip the score dough over, and roll it up into a log. Make sure to close the seam and the edges, and then twist it to form a crescent shape.
  11. Let the dough rise again for another 30 mins. While waiting, preheat your oven to 180°C.
  12. Brush the top of your bread with the egg wash, and then bake it for 15-17 minutes. Let it cool, then serve and enjoy!
